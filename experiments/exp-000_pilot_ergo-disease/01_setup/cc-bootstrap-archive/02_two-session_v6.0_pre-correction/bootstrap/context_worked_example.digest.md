# Digest: Ergothioneine_CSE_Petrovic_2025_CellMetab.pdf
**Identity:** Ergothioneine improves healthspan of aged animals by enhancing cGPDH activity through CSE-dependent
persulfidation · Petrovic, Filipović et al. · *Cell Metabolism* 37, 542–556 · 2025 · DOI 10.1016/j.cmet.2024.12.008 ·
30 pages · pages covered: 1–30.

## Synopsis
The paper shows that ergothioneine (ET/EGT), a diet-derived thione, extends lifespan and healthspan in *C. elegans* and
improves muscle health in aged rodents, and traces the mechanism: ET is not merely an antioxidant but acts as an
**alternative substrate for cystathionine γ-lyase (CSE/CTH)**, driving production of hydrogen sulfide (H₂S). The H₂S in
turn **persulfidates** target proteins, and persulfidation of **cytosolic glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase (cGPDH/
GPD1)** boosts its activity, raising **NAD⁺** and improving mitochondrial/muscle function in aged animals.

## Objective
Determine whether ET's healthspan benefit is antioxidant-only or has a defined enzymatic mechanism, and identify the
molecular pathway from dietary ET to improved aging phenotypes.

## Methods
- *C. elegans* lifespan + healthspan assays (movement, pharyngeal pumping) ± ET.
- Genetic dependence: CSE/CTH loss-of-function (worm cth-2 / mammalian CTH) to test whether benefits require CSE.
- Biochemistry: ET as CSE substrate → H₂S measurement; protein **persulfidation** assays (dimedone-switch).
- Target ID: cGPDH/GPD1 persulfidation site mapping; enzyme-activity assays on wild-type vs persulfidation-null mutant.
- Metabolite readouts: NAD⁺ levels; downstream muscle-health assays in aged rats.

## Key findings
- ET extends lifespan and improves healthspan (movement, pumping) in *C. elegans* (p.3, Fig.1).
- ET is a genuine **substrate for CSE**, and CSE turns it into **H₂S** (p.6, Fig.2) — a defined enzymatic route, not
  generic radical scavenging.
- The lifespan/healthspan benefit is **CSE-dependent**: removing CSE abolishes ET's benefit (p.8, Fig.3).
- ET raises **NAD⁺** and improves muscle health, via persulfidation of **cGPDH** increasing its activity (p.10, Fig.4).
- The effect generalizes beyond worms to aged rodent muscle (p.10+, Fig.4).

## Figures & schematics
- **Fig 1 (p.3): "ET improves lifespan and healthspan of C. elegans."** Survival curves + healthspan readouts (movement,
  pharyngeal pumping) for ET-treated vs control worms. Shows ET shifts survival right and preserves function with age —
  establishes the phenotype the rest of the paper explains. Load-bearing: this is the effect to be mechanistically
  accounted for.
- **Fig 2 (p.6): "Ergothioneine is a substrate for CSE to produce H₂S."** Enzyme-kinetics / H₂S-production data with CSE
  acting on ET. Demonstrates the first mechanistic step — ET → (CSE) → H₂S — i.e. ET plugs into transsulfuration
  chemistry rather than acting only as an antioxidant. Pivot from phenotype to mechanism.
- **Fig 3 (p.8): "Lifespan and healthspan benefits of ET treatment are CSE dependent."** Lifespan/healthspan of ET ±
  functional CSE (loss-of-function genotype). ET's benefit disappears without CSE → proves CSE is necessary, ruling out
  a CSE-independent antioxidant explanation. This is the causal linchpin figure.
- **Fig 4 (p.10): "Ergothioneine increases the NAD⁺ levels and improves muscle health…"** persulfidation of cGPDH →
  higher cGPDH activity → higher NAD⁺ → improved muscle health (incl. aged rodent data). Closes the chain from H₂S to the
  aging phenotype and shows cross-species relevance.

## Mechanism / model
Dietary **ET** → alternative substrate for **CSE** → **H₂S** production → **persulfidation of cGPDH (GPD1)** → increased
cGPDH activity → increased **NAD⁺** → improved mitochondrial/muscle function → extended healthspan (worms) and better
aged-muscle health (rodents). CSE is necessary (Fig.3); cGPDH persulfidation is the effector step (Fig.4).

## Limitations & open questions
- Dose/bioavailability of ET across tissues in mammals not fully resolved here.
- Whether other persulfidation targets contribute alongside cGPDH.
- Long-term mammalian lifespan (vs healthspan/muscle) not established in this paper.
- Relationship to the parallel ET→MPST→mitochondria axis (companion literature) not integrated here.
