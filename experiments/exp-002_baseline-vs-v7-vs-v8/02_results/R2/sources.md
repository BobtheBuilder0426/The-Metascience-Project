## My own analysis of the cited AnAge data *[analysis]*

| Result | Method | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum lifespan spans 100-fold across mammals | min/max, quality-filtered | vole 2.1 yr → bowhead 211 yr (n=1,003) |
| Maximum lifespan spans 2,450-fold across vertebrates | min/max, quality-filtered | goby 0.16 yr → Greenland shark 392 yr (n=3,924) |
| Body mass explains ~50% of mammalian lifespan variance | OLS log-log | log10(ML)=0.159·log10(mass)+0.674, r=0.705, n=997 |
| Age-at-maturity predicts lifespan after mass correction (mammals) | partial correlation, Fisher-z CI | r=+0.65 [+0.61,+0.69], n=801, p≈1e-97 |
| Growth-rate constant predicts lifespan after mass correction (mammals) | partial correlation | r=−0.37 [−0.48,−0.25], n=215, p≈2e-8 |
| Age-at-maturity predicts lifespan after mass correction (birds) | partial correlation | r=+0.36 [+0.30,+0.42], n=807, p≈7e-26 |
| Growth-rate signal vanishes after mass correction (birds) — *honest null* | partial correlation | r=−0.04 [−0.16,+0.07], n=298, p=0.46 |
| MRDT (direct aging-rate) tracks age-at-maturity after mass correction | partial correlation | r=+0.85 [+0.62,+0.94], n=17, p≈2e-5 |
| Relationship partly survives phylogeny | order-level aggregation | r=+0.53, 20 orders, p=0.017 |
