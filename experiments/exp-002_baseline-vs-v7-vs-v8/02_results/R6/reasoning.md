# Reasoning trace — how the open-loop set-point-drift hypothesis was derived and tested

This document records the full chain from evidence to conclusion, the candidate comparison, the methods behind each computed number, and the pilot design. Every identifier here was verified through live literature and chemistry connectors (see `sources.json`, `claim_evidence_ledger.csv`).

---

## 1. Strategy: derive the mechanism from the evolutionary constraint, don't bolt them together

A deep answer to "why does aging occur?" must fuse the ultimate (evolutionary) and proximate (mechanistic) questions. My strategy was to find **one premise that generates the proximate process as a logical consequence of the ultimate one**, then subject it to a pre-declared seven-constraint test (C1–C7) with a five-level classification (predicts / explains / compatible / outside-scope / contradicts) and four kill-criteria (the hard cases: naked mole-rat non-Gompertzian mortality; reprogramming age-reset; DR/mTOR cross-kingdom conservation; embryonic ground-zero). I committed in advance to abandon my favored direction if a rival scored higher or if it needed bolt-on assumptions to survive a kill-criterion.

## 2. The derivation chain (evidence → claim)

1. **Force of selection declines with age** to ~0 after reproduction ends (Hamilton 1966, PMID 6015424; Medawar 1952; Williams 1957, DOI 10.1111/j.1558-5646.1957.tb02911.x). *Strong, foundational, undisputed.*
2. Selection tunes any control system **only against states it can act on**. A homeostatic loop's "reference"/set-point is itself a selected parameter.
3. Therefore **there is no selected reference for the post-reproductive body.** The set-points are calibrated only through the age window where selection still has force.
4. A controller that loses its reference does not stop — it **continues its last trajectory**. The body's last trajectory before maturity is the *developmental* program, which is intrinsically multiplicative/directional (growth, differentiation).
5. Hence post-maturity physiology **runs open-loop, extrapolating development** — this is precisely the "quasi-program"/hyperfunction phenomenology (Blagosklonny 2006, PMID 17012837), now with an evolutionary *cause* rather than a redescription.
6. **Aging = accumulating control error** of this drift. Molecular damage accumulates as a downstream consequence (maintenance is one of the drifting set-points), which is why damage is real but not primary.

**What is derived vs. what is posited.** Only steps 1–3 follow from evolutionary theory: Hamilton's result licenses "the youthful reference is *unselected* post-maturity" and nothing more. Steps 4–6 add three explicit physiological **posits** that are the hypothesis's falsifiable, non-tautological content — not theorems: **(P1)** the set-point is a *maintained* quantity that decays once unselected (it could instead be fixed chemistry that never drifts); **(P2)** it drifts *along the developmental direction* rather than randomly (the directional, quasi-program claim); **(P3)** damage is *downstream* of the resulting control error rather than an independent forcing term. I formalize these in a small control model (M1–M5, state x, reference r, input u=K(r−x), damage D, hazard h) in `causal_model_spec.txt`. Making P1–P3 explicit is what lets the hypothesis be wrong: if set-points do not drift, or drift randomly, or damage is primary, the model fails. The move that makes this novel rather than a synonym for hyperfunction is the step-3→6 reframing of aging as a **control error with a reference that no longer exists** — which generates a specific claim about *reversibility structure* (§5).

## 3. The decisive, de-circularized intervention test (C5)

**Circularity risk:** "interventions that work target aging pathways, therefore those pathways cause aging" is circular if the intervention set is chosen by mechanism. **Fix:** use the NIA Interventions Testing Program (ITP) — a frozen, outcome-defined set tested under one 3-site UM-HET3 protocol with statistically matched negatives (study design: Miller 2007, PMID 17578509).

**Procedure (pre-registered order):**
1. Froze the intervention list by **outcome only** (extends median lifespan: yes/no), from primary ITP reports — positives: rapamycin (PMID 19587680), acarbose/17α-estradiol/NDGA (PMID 24245565), aspirin+NDGA (PMID 18631321), Protandim (PMID 27312235), glycine (PMID 30916479), canagliflozin (PMID 32990681); negatives: resveratrol/green-tea/curcumin/oxaloacetate/MCT (PMID 22451473), methylene blue (PMID 24245565), metformin-alone/fish-oil/UDCA (PMID 27312235). N=17 (8 positive, 9 negative).
2. Assigned each a **mechanism direction from ChEMBL curated mechanism-of-action, before scoring outcomes**: A = attenuates anabolic/nutrient/inflammatory signaling; B = activates hormetic stress response; C = supplies antioxidant/damage-buffer/dietary bulk. (ChEMBL confirmed NDGA acts via IGF1R/ERBB2 — growth signaling — not only 5-lipoxygenase, CHEMBL313972; rapamycin→FKBP1A/mTOR CHEMBL413; metformin→complex-I/AMPK CHEMBL1431.)
3. **Test:** does direction predict outcome? Fisher one-sided OR=∞, p=3.7×10⁻⁴; permutation of labels p=4×10⁻⁴; **leave-one-out held-out accuracy = 94%** vs 53% majority base-rate (perm p=4×10⁻⁴). Signaling/hormetic 8/9 positive; antioxidant/dietary **0/8**. (`convergence_result.json`, Fig 1.)

4. **Outcome-blind re-coding of the mechanism labels.** Because the mechanism labels are the load-bearing step, I checked their reproducibility: a separate classifier re-assigned each compound to A/B/C from its ChEMBL pharmacology **with the lifespan outcome withheld**. It reproduced the labels **16/17 (94%)**, exact and binary (`independent_recode.json`), and re-running the convergence on those labels still separates the classes: signaling/hormetic 8/10 vs antioxidant 0/7, Fisher p=1.9×10⁻³, held-out 88%, perm p=2.9×10⁻³ (`independent_convergence.json`). The single disagreement — resveratrol coded *hormetic* (putative SIRT1 activator) rather than *antioxidant* — moves a lifespan-**negative** into the hormetic class, which if anything *weakens* the rule, yet it survives at p<0.01. This confirms the labels are not outcome-driven; a fully literature-independent human coding study would strengthen it further and remains future work.

**Why this discriminates theories:** the free-radical/damage theory (Harman 1956, PMID 13332224) predicts the antioxidant class *should* be the winners. It is the clean loser (0/8). The signaling-lowering class — predicted to win by any control-error/hyperfunction account — wins 8/9.

**Honest exceptions, deliberately retained:** (a) metformin-alone (a nutrient-sensing/AMPK drug, class A) **failed** at 0.1% (PMID 27312235). I kept it in class A, where it *costs* the rule one error, rather than reclassifying it to protect the result — the 94%/1-error figure already includes this miss. (b) **Aspirin** is not a clean replicated positive: extended male lifespan in the 2008 cohort (PMID 18631321, p=0.01) but "no benefits… at either of two doses" in the 2019 cohort (PMID 30916479). Robustness (`aspirin_sensitivity.json`, seed 7): baseline (aspirin=positive) 8/9 vs 0/8, Fisher p=3.7×10⁻⁴, held-out 94.1%, perm p=4×10⁻⁴; **drop aspirin** 7/8 vs 0/8, Fisher p=7.0×10⁻⁴, held-out 93.8%, perm p=1.4×10⁻³; **aspirin=negative (worst case)** 7/9 vs 0/8, Fisher p=1.9×10⁻³, held-out 88.2%, perm p=2.3×10⁻³. All variants stay significant at p<0.01, so the headline does not depend on aspirin's disputed status. (Methylene blue: correctly a *median-lifespan* null but had a marginal +6% female **maximum**-lifespan effect, P=0.004, PMID 24245565 — footnoted, not counted as a positive.)

**Matched-null STRING check (why the convergence is functional, not structural).** A STRING comparison of positive- vs negative-intervention protein targets (`string_compare.json`): positive targets (MTOR, FKBP1A, MGAM, SI, ESR1, ALOX5, IGF1R, ERBB2, PTGS1/2, NFE2L2, KEAP1, SLC5A2) — 13 nodes, 27 edges, **max degree 8, mean 4.15, density 0.35, no dominant hub**; negative-intervention protein targets (SIRT1, PRKAA1/2, NR1H4) — 4 nodes, 4 edges, density 0.67. The positives are **not** more tightly connected than the negatives (if anything less, before the size confound). **Conclusion:** PPI topology does **not** separate lifespan-extenders from failures, so a PPI-hub convergence would be misleading. The convergence is on a **functional principle (lower the set-point error), not one molecular hub**. This is why the mechanism-direction test (ChEMBL MoA class A/B/C), not a network-hub test, is the correct discriminator and the basis of Fig 1.

## 4. Candidate comparison (C1–C7 × five-level classification)

I scored six currencies into the common template (`candidate_scores.json`, Fig 3): (i) stochastic damage, (ii) somatic mutation, (iii) epigenetic-information loss, (iv) hyperfunction, (v) disposable soma, (vi) open-loop drift.

**Scoring, with a deliberate guard against inflating my own candidate.** "Predicts" (P) is reserved for constraints where the hypothesis makes a prediction that does **not** rely on an assumption already built into the model. Only two qualify: **C1** (a direct logical derivation from Hamilton, modulo the three explicit posits P1–P3 above) and **C5** (the held-out ITP test, p=4×10⁻⁴). **C2 and C3 are "explains," not "predicts,"** because the Fig-2 generative model *assumes* an exponential per-individual hazard: it demonstrates that open-loop drift is *sufficient* for Gompertz+plateau but does not *derive* the mortality law, and the plateau is a generic frailty effect not unique to drift. **C4, C6, C7** are "explains" because their evidence is shared with rival theories and does not uniquely discriminate. Explanatory-fit scores (P=+2, E=+1, C=0, X=−2, minus an added-assumption penalty): damage −3, somatic −3, epigenetic-noise 2, disposable-soma 4, hyperfunction 4, **open-loop drift 8** (codes P,E,E,E,P,E,E; 2 predicts, 5 explains, 0 contradictions). The margin over the best rival (hyperfunction, 4) rests on the evolutionary derivation, the one data-earned prediction, and the absence of any contradicted constraint — not on the shared explanatory constraints.

Damage and somatic-mutation theories are the only ones that *contradict* constraints (C5 antioxidant nulls; C6 reset), and they fail ≥2 kill-criteria without bolt-ons. Open-loop drift passes all four kill-criteria without bolt-ons. Its margin over the best rival (hyperfunction, fit 4) rests on the evolutionary derivation (C1), the one data-earned prediction (C5), and zero contradictions — *not* on the mortality/cross-species constraints, which are shared explanatory (sufficiency) accounts.

**Honest read:** hyperfunction (iv) is the strongest rival and shares most predictions. Open-loop drift's advantage is (a) it *derives* hyperfunction from the evolutionary constraint rather than positing it, (b) it forces hallmark *co-occurrence* (C4), and (c) it makes the reversibility-structure prediction (§5). It is **not** claiming a new molecular mechanism.

## 5. The novel prediction and why rivals differ

"Aging is reversible" does **not** by itself discriminate this hypothesis, because two rivals also predict recoverability. The discriminator has to be stated precisely (see `rival_prediction_table.csv` for the steelmanned per-theory predictions):
- **Irreversible-lesion theories** — stochastic damage (Harman 1956), somatic mutation (Cagan 2022, DOI 10.1038/s41586-022-04618-z), disposable soma (Kirkwood 1977): function cannot be restored without removing the lesion. Restoration **tracks damage clearance**.
- **Epigenetic-information loss** (Yang 2023, DOI 10.1016/j.cell.2022.12.027, correction 10.1016/j.cell.2024.01.049; Lu 2020, PMID 33268865): function **is** restorable — but by **rewriting the epigenome and resetting the clock**. Lu 2020 states the thesis directly: the question is "whether older individuals retain the information needed to restore these patterns… and whether this could improve tissue function." Restoration **tracks clock reset**.
- **Hyperfunction** (Blagosklonny 2006): restorable by lowering growth signaling. Open-loop drift **shares** this — it is a sibling, not discriminated. I explicitly do *not* claim to beat hyperfunction empirically here; my edge over it is the evolutionary derivation and unification, not a distinct prediction.
- **Open-loop drift:** the deficit is a control error; **restoring the reference INPUT rescues function at a fixed epigenetic clock and fixed damage load** — no clock reset, no lesion removal. Restoration **precedes and is independent of** both.

The **fixed-clock/fixed-damage condition** is the sharp discriminator: it splits open-loop drift from information-loss (which needs the clock to move) and from all lesion theories (which need damage removed). Closest empirical prior art is the *resilience/recovery-rate* literature (Pyrkov 2021, PMID 34035236, "progressive loss of resilience predicts human lifespan"; Cohen 2018 dysregulation PMID 29939206; critical slowing, Olde Rikkert 2016, PMID 26765499), the **"mallostasis" mean-reverting-set-point model** (Cohen group, PMID 38092834, which operationalizes homeostasis as a multivariate mean-reverting process whose equilibria drift with age — the closest formal precedent to set-point drift), and **Dilman's neuroendocrine "elevation" model** (PMID 381162, developmental program continuing post-maturity via hypothalamic set-point loss — a 1970s predecessor). These establish the drift *correlation*; **my added claim is causal and about ordering:** resilience is *recoverable by input restoration at fixed clock and fixed damage*. ("Cybernetic theory of aging" has no clean primary PubMed entry — I flag it as a term without a locatable anchor rather than cite it.) That ordering is the held-out difference no existing study resolves.

## 6. The pilot — smallest test that could falsify the core claim

**Model system.** Replicatively-aged primary human dermal fibroblasts (late-passage, senescence-enriched) — cheap, standard, no animals. Optionally aged human myotubes or iPSC-derived cells if available.

**Design — necessity + rescue at fixed damage.**
- **Perturbation:** acute, reversible lowering of a growth/nutrient set-point — a rapamycin pulse (mTORC1↓), with an orthogonal arm (torin1 or amino-acid restriction) to avoid drug-specific artifacts.
- **Functional readout (the "set-point"):** autophagic flux (LC3-II turnover ± bafilomycin), a proteostasis reporter (e.g. luciferase refolding), and/or nucleocytoplasmic transport integrity — readouts known to decline with cellular age and to respond fast.
- **Damage *and the epigenetic clock* held fixed and *measured, not manipulated*:** γH2AX foci (DNA damage), lipofuscin autofluorescence, **and a DNA-methylation or transcriptomic age clock**, quantified at each timepoint. The clock readout is the specific element that lets this discriminate open-loop drift from **epigenetic-information loss**, not just from damage theories.
- **Timing is the discriminator:** sample hours→days.
  - *Drift supported* if function recovers **within hours–days while both damage markers and the clock are unchanged** (rescue by input alone).
  - *Information-loss supported* if function recovers only as/after the clock resets; *damage theory supported* if only as/after damage markers fall.
- **Re-drift arm:** withdraw the perturbation → function should decay back toward the aged set-point (tests that the reference, not a one-way repair, is doing the work).

**Controls:** young fibroblasts (ceiling), vehicle (floor), a pure antioxidant arm (N-acetylcysteine) predicted by drift theory to **not** rescue the functional set-point — a second, independent discriminator against damage theory.

**Feasibility.** All reagents and assays are off-the-shelf; readouts are plate-based. A single researcher with basic tissue-culture and imaging/plate-reader access can complete the core necessity+rescue arm in **~8 weeks** (≈2 weeks cell expansion/aging QC, ≈4 weeks perturbation timecourses in triplicate, ≈2 weeks imaging/analysis). Budget is ordinary consumables. It is falsifying because it pits *timing of functional rescue* against *timing of damage change* — the one axis on which the theories make opposite predictions.

## 7. Methods notes (reproducibility)

- **Literature/identifier verification:** PubMed (`search_articles` → `get_article_metadata`) and OpenAlex (`openalex_get_work` by DOI) via connectors; classic pre-PubMed works resolved by DOI. Metadata key is `identifiers.pmid`; DOI is top-level.
- **Drug→target mapping:** ChEMBL `compound_search` → `get_mechanism` (curated mechanism_of_action).
- **Convergence statistics:** Fisher exact (one-sided), 20,000-label permutation, and leave-one-out held-out accuracy with a 5,000-permutation null (`scipy`, seed 7). N=17.
- **Generative model:** per-individual hazard h(t)=a·e^{bt}; Gamma-distributed drift rate b across individuals (frailty); cohort survival S(t)=mean_i exp(−∫h_i); cohort hazard = −d ln S/dt. One parameter (mean b) toggles Gompertz+plateau vs flat. Sufficiency demonstration, not a fit to primary life tables (SSA table was not machine-accessible; the fit is documentation, not a decision-driver).
- **Causal model:** the derivation is written as an explicit control model (`causal_model_spec.txt`): state x, moving reference r, feedback input u=K(r−x), downstream damage D, hazard h∝‖x−x_young‖ — separating the evolutionary consequence (reference unselected) from the three added physiological posits P1–P3.
- **Independent mechanism re-coding:** the A/B/C mechanism labels were regenerated by a separate classifier from ChEMBL pharmacology **blind to lifespan outcome** (`independent_recode.json`); 16/17 concordance with hand-coding, and the convergence re-run on the independent labels stays p<0.01 (`independent_convergence.json`).
- **Rival predictions:** each rival theory's strongest published reversibility prediction tabulated in `rival_prediction_table.csv`, showing the fixed-clock/fixed-damage condition splits open-loop drift from the lesion and information-loss camps but not from hyperfunction.
- **STRING network:** `get_string_network`, human, score≥400, for **both** the positive-target set and the negative-intervention target set (matched comparison; used to *reject* a naive single-hub claim — see `string_compare.json`).
- **Citation verification:** every PMID confirmed to exist and match its claim via PubMed `get_article_metadata` (title + abstract read for scope-critical claims); every load-bearing DOI confirmed via OpenAlex `openalex_get_work` (including that Yang 2024 is typed as an *erratum*, and Cagan 2022 PMID 35418684 / Pyrkov 2021 PMID 34035236 exist with matching titles).
