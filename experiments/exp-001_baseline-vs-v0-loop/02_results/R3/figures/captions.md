# Figure captions

**fig1_hypothesis_schematic.png** — The cytosolic-redox / lactate-threshold hypothesis. Dietary EGT, concentrated in working muscle by exercise-induced PGC-1alpha->SLC22A4, is handled by two H2S-generating enzymes (cytosolic CSE, per Petrovic; mitochondrial MPST, per Sprenger) that converge on low-level, compartmentalised sulfane sulfur. This drives (i) persulfidation-activation of cytosolic GPDH at Cys243 and (ii) SQR-mediated electron entry to CoQ, both of which speed cytosolic NADH re-oxidation. The proposed net effect (orange band) is a rightward shift of the lactate threshold — the whole-animal determinant of running speed. Footnote states the H2S hormesis caveat.

**fig2_predicted_lactate_threshold.png** — Predicted primary readout. Blood lactate vs treadmill speed for control vs EGT. The hypothesis predicts EGT shifts the curve right (higher speed at the 4 mmol/L OBLA reference), distinguishing it from a pure VO2max/biogenesis model. Conceptual prediction, not measured data.

**fig3_two_paper_synthesis.png** — Side-by-side of the two provided papers (model, enzyme, EGT's role, effector, downstream node, performance result, whether lactate/NAD+ were measured, time-to-effect) and the shared logic. Highlights the specific gap the hypothesis fills: neither paper tested the lactate threshold in a running animal.

**fig4_pilot_design_power.png** — First pilot. Left: 2x2 design (diet x enzyme genotype) in young mice, n~12/group; the greyed KO row is the falsification linchpin (predicted loss of the EGT effect). Right: two-sample t-test power; the expected effect (d~1.25) needs n~12/group for 80% power (dashed = 90%).
