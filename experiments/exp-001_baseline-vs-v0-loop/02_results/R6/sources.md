# Sources

Every source drawn on for the Cofactor Ratchet hypothesis, grouped by role. All DOIs were verified against OpenAlex (by search and by direct DOI lookup with title confirmation) during this work — none are reconstructed from memory. Publication years are those returned by the verification lookup (online-first year where it differs from the print issue). Two pre-DOI classics (Oparin 1938, Haldane 1929) are cited as the monograph/essay they are.

**Total: 27 sources.** Verification method and per-source checks are recorded in `process_trace.json`.

## Foundational / classic

- J. B. S. Haldane (1929). *The Origin of Life*. The Rationalist Annual. **Identifier:** (no DOI; classic essay). — *'Hot dilute soup' concept.*
- A. I. Oparin (1938). *The Origin of Life*. Macmillan (transl. S. Morgulis). **Identifier:** ISBN: (no DOI; classic monograph). — *Coacervate hypothesis: droplets as pre-cells.*

## Cofactors as molecular fossils (the pivot)

- H. B. White III (1976). *Coenzymes as fossils of an earlier metabolic state*. Journal of Molecular Evolution. **DOI:** [10.1007/BF01732468](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01732468). — *Nucleotide moiety of cofactors = RNA-world relic.*
- A. D. Goldman, B. Kaçar (2021). *Cofactors are remnants of life's origin and early evolution*. Journal of Molecular Evolution. **DOI:** [10.1007/s00239-020-09988-4](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00239-020-09988-4). — *Modern synthesis of cofactor-fossil hypothesis.*

## Compositional inheritance (GARD) and its limits

- D. Segrè, D. Ben-Eli, D. Lancet (2000). *Compositional genomes: prebiotic information transfer in mutually catalytic noncovalent assemblies*. PNAS. **DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.97.8.4112](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.97.8.4112). — *GARD model; composomes.*
- V. Vasas, E. Szathmáry, M. Santos (2010). *Lack of evolvability in self-sustaining autocatalytic networks constrains metabolism-first scenarios for the origin of life*. PNAS. **DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.0912628107](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0912628107). — *Key critique: compositional systems have limited heritable variation/evolvability.*
- V. Vasas, C. Fernando, M. Santos, S. Kauffman, E. Szathmáry (2012). *Evolution before genes*. Biology Direct. **DOI:** [10.1186/1745-6150-7-1](https://doi.org/10.1186/1745-6150-7-1). — *Conditions for pre-genetic evolvability (autocatalytic cores).*
- D. Lancet, R. Zidovetzki, O. Markovitch (2018). *Systems protobiology: origin of life in lipid catalytic networks*. J. Royal Society Interface. **DOI:** [10.1098/rsif.2018.0159](https://doi.org/10.1098/rsif.2018.0159). — *GARD review; lipid catalytic networks.*

## Heredity limits: the Eigen error threshold

- M. Eigen (1971). *Selforganization of matter and the evolution of biological macromolecules*. Die Naturwissenschaften. **DOI:** [10.1007/BF00623322](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00623322). — *Error threshold; L_max ~ 1/(1-q).*

## RNA world and molecular cooperation

- P. G. Higgs, N. Lehman (2014). *The RNA World: molecular cooperation at the origins of life*. Nature Reviews Genetics. **DOI:** [10.1038/nrg3841](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrg3841). — *RNA-world cooperation; representative RNA-first framework (Table 2).*

## Active coacervate droplets: growth, division, self-maintenance

- M. Abbas, W. P. Lipiński, J. Wang, E. Spruijt (2021). *Peptide-based coacervates as biomimetic protocells*. Chemical Society Reviews. **DOI:** [10.1039/D0CS00307G](https://doi.org/10.1039/D0CS00307G). — *Coacervate protocell review.*

## Coacervates concentrate and enhance nucleic-acid chemistry

- R. R. Poudyal, R. Guth-Metzler, A. J. Veenis, et al. (2019). *Template-directed RNA polymerization and enhanced ribozyme catalysis inside membraneless compartments formed by coacervates*. ## The heredity handoff: in-droplet / ordered-phase oligonucleotide ligation

- K. Adamala, J. W. Szostak (2013). *Nonenzymatic template-directed RNA synthesis inside model protocells*. Science. **DOI:** [10.1126/science.1241888](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1241888). — *Copying chemistry inside compartments.*
- T. P. Fraccia, G. P. Smith, G. Zanchetta, et al. (2015). *Abiotic ligation of DNA oligomers templated by their liquid crystal ordering*. Nature Communications. **DOI:** [10.1038/ncomms7424](https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms7424). — *Physical ordering (crowding) templates non-enzymatic ligation.*
- T. Z. Jia, T. P. Fraccia (2020). *Liquid crystal peptide/DNA coacervates in the context of prebiotic molecular evolution*. Crystals. **DOI:** [10.3390/cryst10110964](https://doi.org/10.3390/cryst10110964). — *Coacervate + LC ordering of nucleic acids.*

## Environmental cycling and selection drivers
